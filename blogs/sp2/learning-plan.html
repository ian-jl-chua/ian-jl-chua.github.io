  1. What is your long term goal or career pathway?

    I am interested in blockchain in the finance field, so hope to gain a bit more understanding on this as I progress with my knowledge on coding. Because I have an understanding in Traditional Finance (TradFi), I am interested to see how Decentralized Finance (DeFi) differs from that.




  2. A description of your strengths and limitations when it comes to learning


    One of my greatest known strengths, is my attention to detail. But turns out that it can be my limitation as well.

    I can be so focused on one thing, or getting one thing done right/properly to the highest standard, that I can miss the "Big Picture". So on one hand, if I know something, I know it really well and in detail. On the other hand, I spent way too much time getting lost in the rabbit hole.

    I remembered something that was mentioned from one of the spiritual teachers, something along the lines of,"You don't need to calculate the vibration of the particles in the strings, or explain how that sound travels to the ear of the listener, to play the guitar. What you need to know is whether you are in tune, or out of tune. If you are out of tune, just adjust your strings so that you are back in tune."

    I am still working out the balance so that I know what is needed and helpful, but not going down the rabbit hole and spending so much time getting caught up in the details.




  3. What skills (non-technical core/human skills) would you like to see developed in yourself while at Dev Academy?


    The ability to communicate and work along side others who might not share the same values and/or goals as me.

    We all want to succeed in life, but success means different things to different people. What I find important to succeed, may not be what someone else finds important.

    After taking the 16 personalities test, I am more aware that sometimes I can be "socially clueless" and other times dismissive of emotions. Knowing how to manage potential conflict or differences, would greatly improve an environment where we need to co-exist.




  4. A commitment to how you will manage your workload in this programme. This should include:
    • An explanation of how you will manage yourself to work productively and safely with other learners, Facilitators and industry/community representatives
    • What you will do when things are building up and starting to get to you.



    Here are a few things I endeavour to do to manage my workload:

    • Making sure to take my breaks - sometimes I get so caught up in a problem or a task that I neglect getting up from my seat to take a break.
    • Daily meditation - making sure I take some time out of my day to mediate, even if it means spending 5 minutes to give myself the time for clarity
    • Empathize with others - whilst this is not an excuse for being hostile, understanding that sometimes when people are hostile or angry, it might be because of something happening in their personal lives.
    • Remember to seek help - sometimes I can get stuck on something for ages and I can get too nervous or feel too stupid to ask for help
    • Remember not to panic - if things build up and get overwhelming, to remember to take a breather and re-assess the situation. Sometimes taking time away from the screen can help rest my mood.



  5. A commitment as to how and where you will seek help in a timely way


    Here are a few things I endeavour to do:

    • Keep track of the time so that I don't get stuck on something for too long, doing this by keeping an eye on the time requirement for time required in the time box.
    • If I am not able to meet a dead line to keep my tutor in the loop. Also to set a time frame on when this can be achieved.
    • If dead lines are not met consistently to set a plan of attack on what to do to prioritize what needs to be done.
    • Utilizing the Discord channel to seek assistance from my tutors.
