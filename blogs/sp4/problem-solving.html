Elegantly solved a problem.

There was a time when my parents found a puddle of water under the laundry sink and could not figure out how big or small this problem would be.

I assessed the situation and was trying to figure out if it was a pipe leak or was it just something not being screwed on properly. We could call a plumber to take a look at it which would have cost a few hundred dollars or I could take a look and see if I can fix it, if not then there's always the option of ringing the plumber.

First step was to clean and dry the area to determine if it is the water piping problem as it flows into the mixer or was the leak coming from the sink. After drying everything I placed a container under the area over night to see. because the process was so gradual we couldn't see the water dripping but we would know the next day how much water was leaking.

This is where I determined it was either the hot or cold water pipe that was flowing in to the mixer and not the sink itself. Using the tools I had (after turning the water off at the main source) I took apart the pipes, dried off everything and assembled them back together. Once again placing a container under the sink overnight to see if the problem has been fixed.

Went to check on it the next morning and the container was completely dry. That was when I determined, it could have been one of the nuts that had not been tightened properly. Because if it was really a break in the pipe, we would have still seen water in the container, or I would need to do further investigation. Expensive pluming bill avoided.

I learned from this experience that it is ok to first try things yourself and to give it a go. Because at the end of the day, if I didn't attempt to fix it, we would have needed to ring a plumber, if I had tried to fix it and made a mess of it, we would have STILL needed to ring a plumber. Either way if the outcome is bad, a plumber would be needed, but if I tried it first, I could potentially save some money from attempting it myself.
