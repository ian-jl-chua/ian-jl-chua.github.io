Sprint 4 Core

  1. Neuroplasticity


    In basic, this is talking about how our brain can change and does change throughout our lives, and that the brain is not hard wired and fixed. Our brain is adaptable, like plastic, hence the term "Neuroplasticity".

    Our brain is like a central hub. Everything we do, experience etc is utilizing different parts of our brain. The more we do a certain task or build a certain pattern, the more it strengthens that particular pathway of the part of the brain that is used. This also means that we can break old habits and create new ones by consistently gearing our brain towards new thoughts or new ways of doing things.

  2. Growth Mindset


    Growth mindset overlaps with neuroplasticity. A growth mindset is understanding that intelligence can be obtain. Putting in the right effort in learning, will help a person get to the goal they are trying to achieve.

    Reading a growth mindset article, it was also mentioned that if effort was praised, it leads to a better outcome, where students are inclined to try harder to achieve a better grade. It also helps build resilience to bounce back after failure.

    I will endeavour to incorporate what I have learned here not just into my learning plan but also for my own personal development. I have noticed tendencies in me to be self critical, and constantly comparing myself to others who are better than me.

    On one hand there is awareness that if I compare myself to others, this helps give me a guide to see where my weaknesses are and where improvements can be made. On the other hand, this can also be quite damaging. I am still in the process of exploring how to strike a balance of a healthy comparison where I view it as an opportunity to improve but at the same time not beat myself up and to allow myself time to grow.
