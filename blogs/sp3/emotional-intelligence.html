Emotional Intelligence

What is Emotional Intelligence?

According to an article in Harvard Business School Online "Emotional intelligence is defined as the ability to understand and manage your own emotions, as well as recognize and influence the emotions of those around you. The term was first coined in 1990 by researchers John Mayer and Peter Salovey, but was later popularized by psychologist Daniel Goleman."

This article talks about 4 components. Self-awareness, Self-management, Social awareness, and Relationship management. Although the wider internet seems to show 5 component, with Empathy and Motivation most likely grouped under the same umbrella as "Relationship management"

How is EQ different to IQ?

According to the internet.

IQ (Intelligence Quotient) measures your ability to:

  • Solve problems with logic
  • Ability to recognize and use language
  • Understanding abstract ideas
  • Plan and strategies
  • Learn and adapt to change

EQ (Emotional Quotient) looks more at your ability to:

  • Empathize with people
  • Awareness emotion on yourself and others
  • Communicate well with others
  • Motivate yourself, withstanding temptations
  • Regulate and adapt your feelings

Why is EQ important?

Understand people around you more, effectively manage conflict, build better relationships. All of which would lead to better mental wellbeing and the ability to help yourself and others around you.
