Identity and Values

Explain a situation where you have made an ethical decision. Discuss how you weighed up the values involved in that decision, the decision you made, and how you reflect on the decision now.

I have been working in the filed of finance for about 5 years. I can't think of a specific situation because I feel that this needs to happen with all situations, with every customer. An example I can use is to deal with loan applications in general.

Whilst I am aware that every company needs to make money, I feel that this can be done ethically too. That means if a loan application is unlikely going to work, I won't tweak the application just to make it work, at the expense of it being detrimental to the customer.

This is because assessing a loan application is very much an area open for interpretation, what someone else thinks can be omitted in an application, might not be something I want to omit when I assess the loan. If a customer's gambling habit is going to affect their loan repayment, this needs to be included into the expense to paint a clearer picture on the customer’s position. 

And this may lead to the loan application being decline, the customer being upset about the outcome, and the company losing a sale. But by not approving this application, I know that I didn’t put the customer in a worse off financial position, and if the declined was explained properly to the customer, they will know what changes are needed to be made in order for us to consider their future application. Instead of a "No" it becomes a "Not now, but here's how."

Describe how your culture and the people around you have influenced your values and identity.

Growing up in a Buddhist background, I am taught at a young age to fear karma, in other words "What goes around, comes around." and "Do unto others, as you would have them do unto you." But as I go through the various stages of adulthood, I also realize that just because I treat others well, doesn't mean that others would treat me well.

So when Karma is incorporated with rebirth, it becomes a situation where "What goes around, comes around, but you may not always be able to experience the fruits of your good deed in this lifetime or you may have to go through certain difficulties because of what you have done in past lifetimes."

There are times that life is hard and I have to remind myself to persevere with good values, even if it means I am the short term loser, because in the long term (maybe not this life) things would work out better. And this ends up changing the outlook I have in terms of values and identity.

With this in mind, my approach in life has also changed. I aim to surround myself with people who share the same (if not similar) values to me, and distance myself from those who don't. But we can't always surround ourselves with people we like and distant ourselves from those who don't, because life is more complex than that. One of the things I hope to learn (in this journey of life), is how to co-exist with people who don't share my values. Because saying something is your value, and actually practicing and living the value, are 2 different things.

Here are some values that are important to me:

  • Sincerity - (I'm ok if people don't like me, I'm just not ok with people pretending to like me)
  • Accountability - (Do what you say, and say what you do)
  • Honesty and Integrity - (Truthfulness is important. But right action, at the right time, at the right place, those are equally important.)

Evaluate your limitations in terms of your learning and career development. How might these affect your learning journey?

I would like to say, my main limitation is being dyslexic. And a minor one being time management, because there are times when I manage well and there are times when I don't.

I think both of those will go hand in hand to brew the perfect storm. Under normal circumstances I would factor a set amount of time into doing a task, but what I can't factor in, is the time it takes for me to first understand how long it would take if I do get stuck.

So 2 examples will be, meal prep and fixing the tap.

With meal preparation it is easy, I have done this task for years and it has become a part of me, so time management isn't an issue because I know exactly how long to allocate for and what needs to be done.

With fixing a tap. I would allocate a set time to do the task, but I can't take into account how long it would take me to figure out what the problem is to begin with. And this could take me a few minutes or a few hours. And this affects my time management because I would over estimate and not get a task completed on time.

Share an example of when you were trying to work productively with others, but there was resistance or tension. Discuss strategies you tried at the time, how effective they were, and your reflections on what other strategies you would try now and why.

This is something I don't really have a clear example for.

For most of my work life, I was not in a position where I needed to manage a team. I have the area that I need to look after and as long as I have taken care of my side of things, I try not to get involved with what others are doing.

From past experience I have been in situations where it was a "Mind your own business" and "You're not the boss of me", so I tend to work in areas where I can work independently so that what others do, don't impact the outcome of my work.

Like the most recent job. I am in a role where customers get booked in to see me for an appointment. This is quite independent in a sense that if I took too long with a customer, my colleagues are not impacted. If I spend a bit of extra time with the customer and I went to lunch late, it is up to me to juggle my lunch break so I can see my next customer on time. It is between me and my customers. This is different to my previous job in a retail environment where, if I'm' late to lunch, my colleague goes late to lunch, and it was a domino effect so the team can be impacted at large.

But often at my various jobs, I would hear people say "Ask for forgiveness, not permission.". This is something interesting that I would apply when I am in a situation that requires me to do things that way.
