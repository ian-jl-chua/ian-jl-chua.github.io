  1. Explain a situation where you have made an ethical decision. Discuss how you weighed up the values involved in that decision, the decision you made, and how you reflect on the decision now.


    I have been working in the filed of finance for about 5 years. I can't think of a specific situation because I feel that this needs to happen with all situations, with every customer. An example I can use is to deal with loan applications in general.

    Whilst I am aware that every company needs to make money, I feel that this can be done ethically too. That means if a loan application is unlikely going to work, I won't tweak the application just to make it work, at the expense of it being detrimental to the customer.

    This is because assessing a loan application is very much an area open for interpretation, what someone else thinks can be omitted in an application, might not be something I want to omit when I assess the loan. If a customer's gambling habit is going to affect their loan repayment, this needs to be included into the expense to paint a clearer picture on the customer’s position. 

    And this may lead to the loan application being decline, the customer being upset about the outcome, and the company losing a sale. But by not approving this application, I know that I didn’t put the customer in a worse off financial position, and if the declined was explained properly to the customer, they will know what changes are needed to be made in order for us to consider their future application. Instead of a "No" it becomes a "Not now, but here's how."




  2. Describe how your culture and the people around you have influenced your values and identity.


    Growing up in a Buddhist background, I am taught at a young age to fear karma, in other words "What goes around, comes around." and "Do unto others, as you would have them do unto you." But as I go through the various stages of adulthood, I also realize that just because I treat others well, doesn't mean that others would treat me well.

    So when Karma is incorporated with rebirth, it becomes a situation where "What goes around, comes around, but you may not always be able to experience the fruits of your good deed in this lifetime or you may have to go through certain difficulties because of what you have done in past lifetimes."

    There are times that life is hard and I have to remind myself to persevere with good values, even if it means I am the short term loser, because in the long term (maybe not this life) things would work out better. And this ends up changing the outlook I have in terms of values and identity.

    With this in mind, my approach in life has also changed. I aim to surround myself with people who share the same (if not similar) values to me, and distance myself from those who don't. But we can't always surround ourselves with people we like and distant ourselves from those who don't, because life is more complex than that. One of the things I hope to learn (in this journey of life), is how to co-exist with people who don't share my values.
